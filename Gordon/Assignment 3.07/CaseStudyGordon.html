Case Study Claim:

“NASA spacecraft travel to far-off destinations in space, but a new mobile app produced by NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Pasadena, California,brings spacecraft to users.”

Explanation:

The specific problem that NASA is trying to address is bringing the inaccessible to the general public. In this case they are "bring[ing] spacecraft[s] to [the] users” around the world.

Most Effective Claim:

The apps feature of displaying the actual sizes of the spacecrafts contribute to debuking the misconceptions that come with learning introductory astronomy.

Explanation:

When using the app you are able to select a whether you want to view the actual size of the spacecrafts or if you would like a minimized version of the spacecraft. During this selection you can also view indepth information on what missions the spacecraft have been on and what the objective of the mission was.

Use of Technology:

The use of augmented reality is the primary technology used in this application.

Explanation:

The jet repulsion labortory collaborated with Google to impliment the augmented reality technology in the app. Googles "ARcore" technology brings accurate representation to the spacecrafts on the app in a three dimensional model.
